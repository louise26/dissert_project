What are cookies and how do they work?  When you access our website(s) and/or app(s) and/or create or log into an online account you agree to our privacy and cookies notice.  If you interact with our website(s) and/or app(s), AT THE RACES will receive and store certain information as “cookies”. Cookies are files which are sent to your browser and stored on your personal computer’s hard disk and are used by most on-line companies. Your browser makes these cookies available every time you visit the website or app again, so it can recognise you and can then tailor what you see on your device.  Cookies are important to make using devices and accessing online information much smoother, plus affect lots of the useful features of websites. AT THE RACES uses cookies to help you use its website, not to store personal identifying information. The use of cookies by AT THE RACES and third parties as described below are for the legitimate interests of AT THE RACES and/or such third parties.  Examples of what cookies are used to achieve:  - Positioning information on a smartphone screen, tablet device or other screen so that you can see the website and use its functionality.  - Keeping you logged in during your visit or enabling you to stream content.  - Some are session cookies which make it possible to navigate through the website smoothly.  How can cookies improve your browsing experience?  - Cookies allow the website(s) or app(s) to remember choices you make, plus your preferences and settings, such as whether you wish to receive marketing information.  - Cookies remember if you’ve filled in a survey, so you will not be asked again.  - Cookies remember if you have visited the site or application previously.  We keep track of what information and links are popular, to help us keep our information relevant and up to date. It is very useful to be able to identify trends of how people navigate our information and when and where ‘error messages’ might originate.  This group of cookies, usually called ‘analytics cookies’ are used to gather this information. The information collected is grouped with the information from everyone else’s cookies. We can then see the overall patterns of usage rather than any one person’s activity. Analytics cookies are used to improve how an application, a website and its pages work. Our application, website and communications you get from us contain small invisible images known as ‘web beacons’ or ‘pixels’. These are used to manage the interaction between you and the online information or email and allows us to access the effectiveness of the communication.  We use ‘affiliate’ cookies. Some of our web based information will contain promotional links to other companies’ sites. If you follow one of these links and then register with them, a cookie is used to tell the other site that you came from our site.  Most modern browsers allow you to see what cookies you have got, and to clear them individually or clear all of them.  You may disable cookies via your browser preferences (most modern browsers have cookies turned on as a default). If you choose to disable cookies you may have difficulties accessing certain areas of the website.  If you interact with third party advertising on our website, the relevant third party advertiser(s) may place cookies on your browser. If you are unable to disable cookies via your browser preferences, please go to the relevant advertiser’s website in order to opt-out of or block their cookies.
